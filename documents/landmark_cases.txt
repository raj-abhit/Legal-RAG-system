
LANDMARK SUPREME COURT AND HIGH COURT CASES - INDIA
===================================================

PART I - FUNDAMENTAL RIGHTS CASES
=================================

1. KESAVANANDA BHARATI v. STATE OF KERALA (1973)
   Citation: AIR 1973 SC 1461
   
   Key Holdings:
   - Established the "Basic Structure Doctrine" - Parliament cannot amend the Constitution 
     to alter its basic structure
   - Basic features include: Supremacy of Constitution, Republican and Democratic form of government,
     Secular character, Separation of powers, Federal character
   - This is the most important constitutional case in India
   - Overruled the Golaknath case partially
   
   Impact: Limited Parliament's power to amend fundamental rights; protects core constitutional values
   
   Relevant Articles: Article 368 (Amendment of Constitution), Part III (Fundamental Rights)


2. MANEKA GANDHI v. UNION OF INDIA (1978)
   Citation: AIR 1978 SC 597
   
   Key Holdings:
   - Expanded the scope of Article 21 (Right to Life and Personal Liberty)
   - Right to life includes right to live with human dignity and all aspects that make life meaningful
   - Procedure established by law must be just, fair and reasonable
   - Any law depriving personal liberty must pass the test of Article 14 and 19
   
   Impact: Transformed Article 21 from a limited right into a comprehensive protection of human rights
   
   Relevant Articles: Article 21, Article 14, Article 19


3. VISHAKA v. STATE OF RAJASTHAN (1997)
   Citation: AIR 1997 SC 3011
   
   Key Holdings:
   - Laid down guidelines for prevention of sexual harassment at workplace
   - Court can legislate when legislature fails to act (judicial legislation)
   - Right to work in safe environment is part of Article 21
   
   Impact: Led to Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace Act, 2013
   
   Relevant Articles: Article 21, Article 14, Article 15, Article 19(1)(g)


4. SHREYA SINGHAL v. UNION OF INDIA (2015)
   Citation: AIR 2015 SC 1523
   
   Key Holdings:
   - Struck down Section 66A of IT Act, 2000 as unconstitutional
   - Protected freedom of speech and expression on internet
   - Vague laws violate Article 14 and Article 19(1)(a)
   
   Impact: Landmark case for digital rights and free speech online
   
   Relevant Articles: Article 19(1)(a), Article 14, Article 21


5. K.S. PUTTASWAMY v. UNION OF INDIA (2017) - Right to Privacy Case
   Citation: (2017) 10 SCC 1
   
   Key Holdings:
   - Right to Privacy is a fundamental right under Article 21
   - Privacy is intrinsic to life and liberty
   - Nine-judge bench decision
   - Overruled MP Sharma and Kharak Singh cases
   
   Impact: Foundation for data protection laws; affected Aadhaar implementation
   
   Relevant Articles: Article 21, Article 14, Article 19


6. NAVTEJ SINGH JOHAR v. UNION OF INDIA (2018) - Section 377 Case
   Citation: (2018) 10 SCC 1
   
   Key Holdings:
   - Decriminalized consensual homosexual acts between adults
   - Section 377 IPC violates Articles 14, 15, 19 and 21
   - Sexual orientation is natural and discrimination based on it is unconstitutional
   
   Impact: Historic victory for LGBTQ+ rights in India
   
   Relevant Sections: Section 377 IPC


7. JOSEPH SHINE v. UNION OF INDIA (2018) - Adultery Case
   Citation: (2018) 15 SCC 1
   
   Key Holdings:
   - Struck down Section 497 IPC (Adultery) as unconstitutional
   - Treats women as property of husband - violates dignity
   - Criminal law cannot be used to regulate matrimonial issues
   
   Impact: Decriminalized adultery; recognized women's agency and dignity
   
   Relevant Sections: Section 497 IPC


PART II - CRIMINAL LAW CASES
=============================

8. BACHAN SINGH v. STATE OF PUNJAB (1980)
   Citation: AIR 1980 SC 898
   
   Key Holdings:
   - Death penalty is constitutional but should be imposed only in "rarest of rare cases"
   - Life imprisonment is the rule; death penalty is the exception
   - Mitigating and aggravating circumstances must be balanced
   
   Impact: Established guidelines for death penalty; still followed today
   
   Relevant Sections: Section 302 IPC, Section 354(3) CrPC


9. D.K. BASU v. STATE OF WEST BENGAL (1997)
   Citation: AIR 1997 SC 610
   
   Key Holdings:
   - Laid down 11 requirements for arrest and detention to prevent custodial violence
   - Right to life includes right against custodial torture
   - Compensation for custodial death or torture
   
   Impact: Landmark case for rights of arrested persons; guidelines incorporated in CrPC
   
   Relevant Articles: Article 21, Article 22


10. ARNESH KUMAR v. STATE OF BIHAR (2014)
    Citation: (2014) 8 SCC 273
    
    Key Holdings:
    - Police must not automatically arrest when offense is punishable for less than 7 years
    - Arrest should not be punitive; liberty is the norm
    - Strengthened Section 41 and 41A CrPC provisions
    
    Impact: Reduced unnecessary arrests; protected personal liberty
    
    Relevant Sections: Section 41 CrPC, Section 41A CrPC


11. MUKESH v. STATE (NCR OF DELHI) (2017) - Nirbhaya Case
    Citation: (2017) 6 SCC 1
    
    Key Holdings:
    - Upheld death penalty for all four convicts in Delhi gang rape case
    - Satisfied "rarest of rare" doctrine
    - Brutal crimes against women demand exemplary punishment
    
    Impact: Led to Criminal Law Amendment Act, 2013; strengthened rape laws
    
    Relevant Sections: Section 376 IPC, Section 302 IPC


PART III - ARTICLE 21 EXPANSION CASES
======================================

12. OLGA TELLIS v. BOMBAY MUNICIPAL CORPORATION (1985)
    Citation: AIR 1986 SC 180
    
    Key Holdings:
    - Right to livelihood is part of right to life under Article 21
    - Cannot deprive someone of livelihood without following just, fair procedure
    - Eviction of pavement dwellers requires reasonable notice
    
    Impact: Expanded Article 21; protected rights of urban poor
    
    Relevant Articles: Article 21, Article 19(1)(e)


13. FRANCIS CORALIE MULLIN v. ADMINISTRATOR, UT OF DELHI (1981)
    Citation: AIR 1981 SC 746
    
    Key Holdings:
    - Right to life includes right to live with human dignity
    - Prison inmates retain fundamental rights except those taken away by law
    - Dignity is the core of Article 21
    
    Impact: Humanized prison conditions; recognized prisoners' rights
    
    Relevant Articles: Article 21


14. MOHINI JAIN v. STATE OF KARNATAKA (1992)
    Citation: AIR 1992 SC 1858
    
    Key Holdings:
    - Right to education is part of right to life under Article 21
    - Capitation fees for medical education are unconstitutional
    - Education is essential for human dignity
    
    Impact: Led to Article 21A (Right to Education) being added to Constitution
    
    Relevant Articles: Article 21, Article 21A (added later)


15. MC MEHTA v. STATE OF TAMIL NADU (1996) - Child Labour Case
    Citation: AIR 1997 SC 699
    
    Key Holdings:
    - Child labour violates Articles 21, 24, and 39
    - Employers must pay compensation for employing children
    - State must ensure education and welfare of rescued children
    
    Impact: Strengthened enforcement of child labour prohibition
    
    Relevant Articles: Article 21, Article 24, Article 39(e)(f)


PART IV - EQUALITY AND DISCRIMINATION CASES
============================================

16. INDRA SAWHNEY v. UNION OF INDIA (1992) - Mandal Commission Case
    Citation: AIR 1993 SC 477
    
    Key Holdings:
    - Upheld 27% reservation for OBCs in government jobs
    - Total reservations cannot exceed 50% (except in extraordinary circumstances)
    - Creamy layer must be excluded from OBC reservations
    - Reservations not applicable in promotions
    
    Impact: Defined reservation policy framework; still governs reservation law
    
    Relevant Articles: Article 16(4), Article 15(4)


17. STATE OF KERALA v. N.M. THOMAS (1976)
    Citation: AIR 1976 SC 490
    
    Key Holdings:
    - Equality means equal treatment of equals and unequal treatment of unequals
    - Compensatory discrimination is not violative of Article 14
    - Reservations are part of equality, not exception to it
    
    Impact: Provided constitutional basis for affirmative action
    
    Relevant Articles: Article 14, Article 16


PART V - SEPARATION OF POWERS AND CONSTITUTIONAL CASES
=======================================================

18. RAM JAWAYA KAPUR v. STATE OF PUNJAB (1955)
    Citation: AIR 1955 SC 549
    
    Key Holdings:
    - Indian Constitution does not have strict separation of powers
    - Broad separation exists but with checks and balances
    - Each organ should respect domain of others
    
    Impact: Defined Indian constitutional framework of separation of powers
    
    Relevant Articles: Articles 50, 121, 211


19. S.R. BOMMAI v. UNION OF INDIA (1994)
    Citation: AIR 1994 SC 1918
    
    Key Holdings:
    - President's Rule under Article 356 is subject to judicial review
    - Secularism is part of basic structure
    - Cannot dismiss state government without material evidence
    
    Impact: Curbed misuse of Article 356; strengthened federalism
    
    Relevant Articles: Article 356, Article 356


20. SUPREME COURT ADVOCATES-ON-RECORD ASSOCIATION v. UNION OF INDIA (2015)
    - NJAC Case
    Citation: (2015) 5 SCC 1
    
    Key Holdings:
    - Struck down 99th Constitutional Amendment creating NJAC
    - Primacy of judiciary in judicial appointments is part of basic structure
    - Independence of judiciary is paramount
    
    Impact: Restored Collegium system for judicial appointments
    
    Relevant Articles: Articles 124, 217


PART VI - ENVIRONMENTAL LAW CASES
==================================

21. MC MEHTA v. UNION OF INDIA (Multiple cases - Environmental Law)
    Various Citations (1986-2000)
    
    Key Holdings:
    - Polluter Pays Principle
    - Precautionary Principle
    - Sustainable Development
    - Right to clean environment is part of Article 21
    
    Impact: Foundation of environmental jurisprudence in India
    
    Relevant Articles: Article 21, Article 48A, Article 51A(g)


22. SUBHASH KUMAR v. STATE OF BIHAR (1991)
    Citation: AIR 1991 SC 420
    
    Key Holdings:
    - Right to pollution-free environment is part of Article 21
    - Right to clean water is fundamental right
    - PIL can be filed for environmental protection
    
    Impact: Expanded scope of Article 21 to environmental rights
    
    Relevant Articles: Article 21


PART VII - PUBLIC INTEREST LITIGATION (PIL) CASES
==================================================

23. S.P. GUPTA v. UNION OF INDIA (1981) - Judges Transfer Case
    Citation: AIR 1982 SC 149
    
    Key Holdings:
    - Liberalized locus standi for PIL
    - Any member of public can approach court for public interest
    - Access to justice is important for rule of law
    
    Impact: Opened doors for PIL in India; democratized justice
    
    Relevant Articles: Article 32, Article 226


24. BANDHUA MUKTI MORCHA v. UNION OF INDIA (1984)
    Citation: AIR 1984 SC 802
    
    Key Holdings:
    - Bonded labour violates Articles 21 and 23
    - State has positive obligation to eliminate bonded labour
    - Courts can issue directions for implementation of laws
    
    Impact: Strengthened enforcement against bonded labour
    
    Relevant Articles: Article 21, Article 23


PART VIII - ADMINISTRATIVE LAW CASES
=====================================

25. A.K. KRAIPAK v. UNION OF INDIA (1969)
    Citation: AIR 1970 SC 150
    
    Key Holdings:
    - Principles of natural justice apply unless expressly excluded
    - Bias vitiates administrative action
    - Even administrative decisions require fairness
    
    Impact: Foundation of administrative law in India
    
    Relevant Articles: Article 14


26. MANEKA GANDHI v. UNION OF INDIA (Administrative Law Aspect)
    See Case 2 above - also important for administrative law
    
    Key Holdings:
    - Procedure must be fair, just and reasonable
    - Article 14 applies to administrative action
    
    Impact: Reformed administrative law; fairness is mandatory


HOW TO USE THIS DOCUMENT
=========================

This document contains landmark cases that have shaped Indian constitutional and criminal law.
When using the RAG system:

1. Ask about specific cases: "What was held in Kesavananda Bharati case?"
2. Ask about legal principles: "What is the basic structure doctrine?"
3. Ask about rights: "What cases expanded Article 21?"
4. Compare cases: "Difference between Maneka Gandhi and ADM Jabalpur?"
5. Ask about impact: "How did Vishaka case affect workplace laws?"

These cases are frequently cited in courts and are essential for legal research.
